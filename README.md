<br>

<details>
<summary><b>Sorting 10,000 photos by hand.</b></summary>
<br>
<blockquote>
<p><b>What annoyed me:</b> Sorting thousands of photos manually after a long event. It drained the energy out of the entire editing room.</p>
<p><b>Why I cared:</b> The time we spent sorting was time we weren't spending actually being creative.</p>
<p><b>What changed:</b> I built an AI-assisted workflow to cull the bad photos automatically. It gave our team days of our lives back.</p>
</blockquote>
</details>

<br>

<details>
<summary><b>Context switching while editing.</b></summary>
<br>
<blockquote>
<p><b>What annoyed me:</b> Having to break my editing flow state to open a browser, search for an asset, download it, and format it.</p>
<p><b>Why I cared:</b> A good edit requires momentum. Context switching kills it.</p>
<p><b>What changed:</b> I wrote a CLI utility that fetches and formats video or audio instantly. The flow stays unbroken.</p>
<p><a href="https://github.com/codeby-arsb/Applications/tree/main/YouTube%20Downloader">[View the Utility]</a></p>
</blockquote>
</details>

<br>

<details>
<summary><b>Losing files in the chaos of an event.</b></summary>
<br>
<blockquote>
<p><b>What annoyed me:</b> Photographers shooting amazing moments, but the media team losing track of assets across massive file transfers.</p>
<p><b>Why I cared:</b> A lost asset is a lost story.</p>
<p><b>What changed:</b> I automated the synchronization scripts. Now photographers shoot, editors edit, and the pipeline quietly handles the rest.</p>
<p><a href="https://github.com/codeby-arsb/Applications/tree/main/Robocopy">[View the Scripts]</a></p>
</blockquote>
</details>
